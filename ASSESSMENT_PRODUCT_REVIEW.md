# Terrnix Carbon Accounting Readiness Assessment — Product Review
**Date:** 2026-07-19
**Reviewer:** External Sustainability Consultant Perspective
**Assessment URL:** https://terrnix.com/carbon-accounting-readiness-assessment/

---

## Executive Summary

The Terrnix Carbon Accounting Readiness Assessment is a well-engineered diagnostic tool that successfully bridges the gap between a simple online quiz and a premium consulting deliverable. The product demonstrates strong technical execution, professional visual design, and thoughtful UX. However, several incremental improvements would elevate it from "very good" to "indispensable" for sustainability professionals.

**Overall Score: 7.8 / 10** — Solid premium product with clear paths to excellence.

---

## 1. Landing Page

**Score: 7 / 10**

### Strengths
- Clean, professional design with clear visual hierarchy
- Value proposition is immediately visible ("Discover how your organisation measures up")
- Benefit bullets clearly communicate what the user receives
- Estimated duration (8-10 minutes) sets appropriate expectations
- Privacy reassurance reduces friction

### Weaknesses
- **Missing social proof**: No logos, testimonials, or "trusted by" indicators. An ESG Director wants to know peers have used this.
- **Weak differentiation**: Headline could be any assessment. Doesn't convey why Terrnix specifically.
- **No preview of output**: Users cannot see what the results look like before committing time. A thumbnail of the report or certificate would increase conversion.
- **CTA is generic**: "Start Assessment" is functional but not compelling. "Get My Maturity Score" or "See Where I Stand" would be stronger.
- **No urgency or scarcity**: No indication of why take it now vs. later.

### Recommendations

| Priority | Improvement | Impact | Effort |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **HIGH** | Add preview image of results page/certificate | High | Low |
| **HIGH** | Strengthen headline with differentiation | High | Low |
| MEDIUM | Add social proof (logos, testimonial quote) | Medium | Low |
| MEDIUM | Improve CTA copy | Medium | Low |
| LOW | Add subtle urgency ("Join 500+ organisations") | Low | Low |

## 2. Questions

**Score: 8 / 10**

### Strengths
- Questions are genuinely relevant to organisational maturity (not trivia)
- Five-point Likert scale provides sufficient granularity
- Help text clarifies intent without being condescending
- Category badges create visual structure
- Progress bar is clear and motivating
- Keyboard navigation works well

### Weaknesses
- **Question 4 (gov-4) and Question 5 (gov-5) overlap**: Both ask about data quality processes and knowledge management. A respondent with systematic QA might be unsure which to rate higher.
- **Question 15 (dat-5) on verification is premature**: It asks about third-party verification before establishing whether basic internal processes exist. This could confuse organisations at Initial/Developing levels.
- **Question 20 (sc3-4) and Question 21 (sc3-5) are adjacent but distinct**: Data quality vs. target-setting for Scope 3 — logical, but the transition feels abrupt without a narrative bridge.
- **No adaptive questioning**: All 25 questions are shown regardless of answers. A respondent who answers "No Scope 3 targets set" on Q21 still sees Q22-Q25 about target management, which feels redundant.
- **Option labels are long**: On mobile, some option text wraps to 3-4 lines, making comparison difficult.

### Recommendations

| Priority | Improvement | Impact | Effort |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **HIGH** | Consolidate overlapping questions (gov-4/gov-5, dat-3/dat-4) | High | Medium |
| **HIGH** | Implement adaptive flow: skip irrelevant questions based on early answers | High | High |
| MEDIUM | Add narrative transitions between categories | Medium | Medium |
| MEDIUM | Shorten option labels for mobile | Medium | Low |
| LOW | Add "Not applicable" option where relevant | Low | Low |

## 3. Results Page

**Score: 8.5 / 10**

### Strengths
- Executive summary is genuinely personalised and context-aware
- Category analysis cards are visually clear and actionable
- Strengths/gaps sections use specific, non-generic language
- 30/60/90-day roadmap provides immediate value
- Recommendations are relevant and link to live content
- Download buttons are prominent and well-designed
- Consultation CTA is appropriately positioned

### Weaknesses
- **Score display dominates**: The large "50" draws attention away from the narrative. A visual balance issue.
- **Category bars lack benchmarks**: No indication of industry average or peer comparison. An ESG Director wants to know "how do we compare?"
- **Roadmap actions are generic**: "Assign a dedicated carbon accounting lead" is correct but not personalised to the specific gap profile. The same actions appear regardless of which categories scored lowest.
- **Recommendations section is buried**: After scrolling through strengths, gaps, and roadmap, the actual resources are at the bottom. These should be more prominent.
- **No sharing mechanism**: No "Share my results" or "Email me a summary" option. Users who want to circulate results internally must download the PDF.
- **Missing: "What does this score mean for my industry?"** Contextualisation would add significant value.

### Recommendations

| Priority | Improvement | Impact | Effort |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **HIGH** | Add industry benchmark comparison (even if static/estimated) | High | Medium |
| **HIGH** | Personalise roadmap actions to specific gap profile | High | Medium |
| MEDIUM | Add "Email me a summary" option | Medium | Medium |
| MEDIUM | Move recommendations higher on the page | Medium | Low |
| MEDIUM | Add LinkedIn sharing option for certificate | Medium | Low |
| LOW | Add industry-specific context to executive summary | Low | High |

## 4. PDF Report

**Score: 7.5 / 10**

### Strengths
- Professional cover page with score preview
- Clear structure: Executive Summary → Categories → Strengths → Gaps → Roadmap
- Terrnix branding is consistent
- Auto-generated filename includes participant name and date
- Methodology and disclaimer provide credibility
- Page numbers and footer on all pages

### Weaknesses
- **Typography is utilitarian**: Helvetica throughout. A premium report would use a professional serif (e.g., Merriweather) for body text and sans-serif for headers.
- **Category scores table is basic**: Standard grid layout. Could use visual bars or colour coding.
- **No charts or visualisations**: A 5-page text report feels dense. Simple bar charts for category scores would improve scannability.
- **Roadmap is text-only**: Bullet points without visual hierarchy. A timeline graphic would be more compelling.
- **No page breaks between major sections**: Content flows continuously, which can feel cramped.
- **Cover page score box is small**: The most important number (50/100) is in a modest box. It should be hero-sized.
- **Missing: Table of contents** For a multi-page report, a TOC improves navigation.

### Recommendations

| Priority | Improvement | Impact | Effort |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **HIGH** | Add simple bar chart visualisations for category scores | High | Medium |
| **HIGH** | Improve cover page score prominence | High | Low |
| MEDIUM | Use better typography (serif body, sans headers) | Medium | Low |
| MEDIUM | Add page breaks between major sections | Medium | Low |
| MEDIUM | Create timeline graphic for roadmap | Medium | High |
| LOW | Add table of contents | Low | Low |

## 5. Certificate

**Score: 7 / 10**

### Strengths
- Landscape A4 format is appropriate for certificates
- Corner decorations add subtle elegance
- Terrnix branding is clear
- Unique certificate ID provides verifiability
- Score and maturity level are prominent
- QR code placeholder is forward-thinking

### Weaknesses
- **Design is generic**: Could be from any online course. No distinctive Terrnix visual identity.
- **Achievement labels are unclear**: "Foundation Achievement" at 50% — does this mean "foundational level" or "achievement in foundations"? Ambiguous.
- **No signature or authorisation**: A certificate without a signature line or issuer name feels less official.
- **QR code is a placeholder**: Currently shows "QR CODE" text. Should either be functional or removed until implemented.
- **No border or seal**: Premium certificates typically have a subtle border, seal, or watermark.
- **Background is flat**: The slate-900 background is professional but plain. A subtle pattern or gradient would elevate it.

### Recommendations

| Priority | Improvement | Impact | Effort |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **HIGH** | Add distinctive Terrnix visual element (pattern, watermark, or seal) | High | Medium |
| **HIGH** | Clarify achievement label naming | High | Low |
| MEDIUM | Add signature line or issuer name | Medium | Low |
| MEDIUM | Implement functional QR code or remove placeholder | Medium | Medium |
| LOW | Add subtle background pattern | Low | Medium |

## 6. UX

**Score: 8 / 10**

### Strengths
- Clean, uncluttered interface
- Progress bar is motivating
- Back button allows correction
- Review screen before submission is excellent
- Mobile-responsive design
- Loading states are clear
- Error handling is graceful

### Weaknesses
- **25 questions feels long**: Even at 8-10 minutes, this is a significant time investment. No indication of progress within categories ("3 of 5 in Governance").
- **No save/resume**: If a user closes the browser, progress is lost. For a 10-minute assessment, this is acceptable but not ideal.
- **Lead capture interrupts flow**: The form appears immediately after the review screen, before results. This is standard practice but feels abrupt without a transition.
- **No estimated time remaining**: The progress bar shows percentage but not "About 3 minutes left."
- **Option selection requires click then next**: Two clicks per question. Some assessments auto-advance on selection, which is faster.
- **Mobile: option text wraps extensively**: Makes comparison harder on small screens.

### Recommendations

| Priority | Improvement | Impact | Effort |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **HIGH** | Add category progress indicator ("Question 3 of 5 in Governance") | High | Low |
| MEDIUM | Implement auto-advance on option selection (with undo) | Medium | Medium |
| MEDIUM | Add "About X minutes remaining" estimate | Medium | Low |
| MEDIUM | Add save/resume via localStorage | Medium | Medium |
| LOW | Smooth transition between review and lead capture | Low | Low |

## 7. Lead Capture

**Score: 7.5 / 10**

### Strengths
- Positioned after completion (correct — not interrupting)
- Clear value exchange: "Unlock your full results, professional report, and verified certificate"
- Required fields are minimal (name, email, consent)
- Optional fields provide segmentation data (company, industry, job title)
- Newsletter consent is separate and optional
- Privacy policy link is present
- Validation is clear and immediate

### Weaknesses
- **Value exchange could be stronger**: "Unlock My Results" is good, but "Get My Personalised Report" or "Download My Certificate" would be more concrete.
- **No preview of what's coming**: Users cannot see a blurred or partial result before submitting details. This increases anxiety.
- **Industry dropdown is basic**: Standard HTML select. A searchable dropdown or typeahead would be better for long lists.
- **No LinkedIn pre-fill**: Allowing LinkedIn OAuth would reduce friction significantly.
- **Consent checkbox is long**: The text wraps and feels legalistic. A shorter version with a modal for full text would be cleaner.

### Recommendations

| Priority | Improvement | Impact | Effort |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **HIGH** | Add blurred preview of results behind the form | High | Medium |
| MEDIUM | Improve CTA copy | Medium | Low |
| MEDIUM | Add LinkedIn pre-fill option | Medium | High |
| LOW | Improve industry dropdown UX | Low | Medium |
| LOW | Shorten consent checkbox with modal for full text | Low | Low |

## Final Recommendation

**Do NOT proceed to Priority 3 (FAQ/Glossary/Guides) yet.**

There are **11 HIGH priority improvements** that would significantly increase the perceived value of the assessment. These are incremental changes, not redesigns, and most are low-to-medium effort.

The highest-impact, lowest-effort wins are:
1. Landing page preview image + stronger headline
2. Certificate visual distinction + label clarity
3. PDF cover page score prominence
4. Category progress indicator
5. Question consolidation

Implementing these would raise the overall score from **7.8 to approximately 8.5+**, moving the product firmly into "premium consulting tool" territory.
