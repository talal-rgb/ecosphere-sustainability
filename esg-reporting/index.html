Who Should Care About ESG Reporting?

ESG reporting is no longer optional. It affects valuation, access to capital, regulatory standing, and brand reputation.

🏢

Public Companies

Listed firms facing mandatory disclosure rules in the EU, UK, US, and beyond. Investor scrutiny is at an all-time high.

🏭

Large Private Firms

CSRD covers many non-listed entities. Banks and insurers increasingly require ESG data for credit and coverage decisions.

💼

Asset Managers

SFDR, TCFD-aligned mandates, and LP demands mean fund managers need clean, comparable ESG data from portfolio companies.

🌍

Supply Chain Leaders

Enterprise buyers now screen suppliers on ESG metrics. A weak score can disqualify you from major contracts.

🚨 Business Risk of Inaction

Companies that fail to disclose face regulatory fines, exclusion from indices, higher cost of capital, and reputational damage. Greenwashing accusations can trigger litigation and consumer boycotts. The EU Green Claims Directive and SEC climate rules raise the stakes further.

💰 Financial Impact of Action

Strong ESG reporters enjoy lower borrowing costs, higher valuation multiples, and better talent retention. ESG-linked loans and sustainability bonds offer pricing advantages. Transparent reporting builds trust with investors, customers, and regulators.

The Major ESG Frameworks

Terrnix helps you navigate and align with the standards that matter to your stakeholders.

Global Standard

GRI (Global Reporting Initiative)

The most widely used sustainability reporting standard. GRI focuses on broad impact disclosures across economic, environmental, and social topics. Ideal for comprehensive sustainability reports and stakeholder communication.

Best for: Large enterprises, NGOs, companies seeking comprehensive impact transparency.

Learn more →
Investor-Focused

SASB (Sustainability Accounting Standards Board)

Industry-specific standards identifying financially material sustainability topics. SASB helps investors compare peer performance on the ESG issues most likely to affect valuation.

Best for: Public companies, investor relations, SEC filing preparation.

Learn more →
Climate Risk

TCFD (Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures)

Focuses on governance, strategy, risk management, and metrics related to climate risks and opportunities. Now embedded into ISSB standards but still widely referenced by investors and regulators.

Best for: Climate risk assessment, scenario analysis, financial materiality.

Learn more →
Consolidated

ISSB (International Sustainability Standards Board)

IFRS S1 and S2 create a global baseline for sustainability disclosures. ISSB consolidates TCFD recommendations and aims to reduce fragmentation. Increasingly referenced by stock exchanges and regulators worldwide.

Best for: Future-proofing disclosures, global capital market alignment.

ESG Compliance Timeline

Key deadlines and regulatory milestones you need to track.

2024 — 2025
EU CSRD Phase 1
Large public-interest entities (500+ employees) must file first sustainability reports under ESRS.
2025 — 2026
CSRD Phase 2 & SEC Rules
Large non-listed companies enter scope. US SEC climate disclosure rules take effect for large filers.
2026 — 2027
CSRD Phase 3 & SMEs
Listed SMEs and small non-complex credit institutions begin reporting. ISSB adoption accelerates globally.
2027 — 2028
Third-Country Subsidiaries
Non-EU companies with significant EU turnover must report on their EU sustainability impacts.
