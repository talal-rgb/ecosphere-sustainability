GHG Protocol: Scopes 1, 2, 3

Scope 1

Direct Emissions

Sources owned or controlled by your organization.

• Stationary combustion (boilers, furnaces)
• Mobile combustion (fleet vehicles)
• Process emissions (chemical reactions)
• Fugitive emissions (refrigeration leaks)
Scope 2

Indirect (Energy)

Purchased electricity, steam, heating, cooling.

• Purchased electricity (grid)
• District heating & cooling
• Steam for industrial processes
• Location-based vs. market-based
Scope 3

Value Chain

All other indirect emissions. Usually 70-90% of total.

• Purchased goods & services (Cat. 1)
• Capital goods (Cat. 2)
• Fuel & energy-related (Cat. 3)
• Upstream/downstream transport
• Business travel & commuting
• Use & end-of-life of sold products

GHG Protocol

Corporate Standard • Scope 2 • Scope 3

Corporate Standard

• 5 principles: Relevance, completeness, consistency, transparency, accuracy
• Organizational boundaries: Equity share, financial control, operational control
• Base year recalculation for structural changes

Scope 2 Guidance

• Location-based method: Grid average factors
• Market-based method: Contractual instruments (RECs, GOs, PPAs)
• Dual reporting required for completeness
• Residual mix for market-based claims

Scope 3 Technical Guidance

• 15 categories defined
• Calculation methods: Spend-based, activity-based, hybrid
• Supplier engagement & data quality
• Science-based targets requirement if >40% of total
