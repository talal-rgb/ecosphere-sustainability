CBAM Compliance Guide

The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism is now in its definitive phase. EU importers face binding obligations, real costs, and tight deadlines. Here's everything you need to comply — and minimise your carbon border tax.

Who Should Care & Why Now

Who is affected?

  • → EU importers of cement, iron & steel, aluminium, fertilisers, electricity, hydrogen
  • → Companies importing >50 tonnes/year of CBAM goods
  • → Supply chain managers, compliance officers, CFOs
  • → Non-EU manufacturers exporting to the EU

Business Risk & Financial Impact

  • ⚠ Operational: Customs clearance blocked without authorized declarant status
  • ⚠ Financial: €65-85/tonne CO2e × embedded emissions = material cost increase
  • ⚠ Competitive: Default values penalise companies without supplier data
  • ⚠ Legal: Non-compliance penalties up to 3× certificate cost + criminal sanctions
