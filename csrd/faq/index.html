CSRD FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions about csrd.

What is CSRD?

The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) is an EU law requiring approximately 50,000 companies to report detailed sustainability information using European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS). It replaces the Non-Financial Reporting Directive (NFRD) and significantly expands reporting requirements.

Does CSRD apply to my company?

CSRD applies to: (1) all large EU companies (>250 employees, >EUR 50M turnover, or >EUR 25M balance sheet), (2) listed EU companies including SMEs, (3) non-EU companies with >EUR 150M EU turnover and an EU branch or subsidiary. Phased implementation from FY2024 to FY2028.

What are ESRS standards?

European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS) are the detailed reporting requirements under CSRD. ESRS 1 and 2 are mandatory for all. Topical standards cover Environment (E1-E5), Social (S1-S4), and Governance (G1). Companies report only on material topics.

What is double materiality?

Double materiality means assessing sustainability topics from two perspectives: (1) impact materiality — how the company affects people and the environment, and (2) financial materiality — how sustainability issues affect the company financially. Both must be disclosed under CSRD.

When is my first CSRD report due?

FY2024 for large public-interest entities already under NFRD. FY2025 for other large companies. FY2026 for listed SMEs. FY2028 for non-EU companies. Reports are due with management reports, typically 6-12 months after year-end.

How much does CSRD compliance cost?

First-year costs range from EUR 30,000 for small companies to EUR 500,000+ for large multinationals. Costs include data infrastructure, consultant support, assurance, and software. Terrnx reduces costs by 30-50% through efficient processes and automation.

What assurance is required for CSRD?

Limited assurance is mandatory from the first reporting year. Reasonable assurance will be required from FY2028. Assurance covers the entire sustainability report and must be provided by a statutory auditor or independent assurance provider.
