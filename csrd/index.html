CSRD is a Board-Level Risk

The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive affects ~50,000 companies. Non-compliance exposes your organization to regulatory fines, higher cost of capital, and exclusion from EU public procurement. The first reports are due in 2025 (for FY2024). If you have not started, the window to close gaps is closing fast.

  • Who: Large EU undertakings, listed SMEs, and large non-EU parents with EU subsidiaries/turnover.
  • Why now: ESRS standards are final. Auditors are booking capacity. Data gaps take 6–12 months to close.
  • Risk: Fines, litigation, credit downgrades, investor withdrawals.
  • Impact: Average first-year compliance cost €150K–€1.2M depending on size and maturity.

Quick Answers

Who should care about CSRD?

Board members, CFOs, CSOs, IR teams, and legal counsel. If your company is a large EU undertaking, a listed SME, or a non-EU parent with significant EU operations, CSRD applies to you—or to your subsidiaries.

Why now?

First reports are due in 2025 (FY2024 data). Auditor capacity is limited. ESRS standards are finalized. Delaying means higher costs, rushed data, and greater assurance risk.

What is the business risk?

Regulatory fines, director liability in some jurisdictions, credit downgrades, investor divestment, and exclusion from EU public procurement contracts.

What is the financial impact?

Direct costs: €150K–€1.2M for first-year compliance. Indirect costs: higher capital costs if ESG data is missing. Upside: improved ESG ratings attract green capital and premium valuations.
