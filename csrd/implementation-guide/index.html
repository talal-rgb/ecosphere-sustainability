CSRD Implementation Guide

A practical 12-step roadmap to CSRD compliance. From gap analysis to your first ESRS report.

Who this is for: Sustainability managers, CFOs, and compliance officers at EU companies or non-EU companies with EU subsidiaries. Timeline: 6-18 months for first report. Cost: EUR 30,000-500,000 depending on size and maturity.

Step 1: Determine Applicability

Check if your company meets CSRD criteria: listed EU companies, large EU undertakings (>250 employees, >EUR 50M turnover, >EUR 25M balance sheet), or non-EU companies with >EUR 150M EU turnover. Timeline varies by category: FY2024 (large public interest entities), FY2025 (large private), FY2026 (listed SMEs), FY2028 (non-EU).

Step 2: Conduct Gap Analysis

Assess current sustainability reporting against ESRS requirements. Identify missing data, processes, and governance. Typical gaps: Scope 3 data, social metrics, governance disclosures, and double materiality assessment methodology.

Step 3: Establish Governance

Assign board-level responsibility for sustainability reporting. Set up cross-functional team (sustainability, finance, legal, HR, operations). Define reporting boundaries and consolidation approach.

Step 4: Perform Double Materiality Assessment

Identify material topics from both impact perspective (how you affect people/planet) and financial perspective (how sustainability affects your finances). ESRS requires disclosure of the assessment process. Use sector-specific guidance from EFRAG.

Step 5: Build Data Collection Infrastructure

Implement systems for collecting environmental data (energy, emissions, water, waste), social data (workforce, human rights, communities), and governance data (board composition, ethics, political influence). Integrate with existing ERP and HR systems where possible.

Step 6: Calculate Environmental Metrics

Apply GHG Protocol for emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3). Use established methodologies for water, waste, pollution, and biodiversity. Document emission factors, activity data, and calculation methodologies. Consider assurance requirements from the outset.

Step 7: Draft ESRS Disclosures

Structure disclosures according to ESRS 1 (General requirements) and ESRS 2 (General disclosures). Add topical standards based on materiality assessment (E1-E5 environment, S1-S4 social, G1 governance). Follow the "comply or explain" principle for datapoints outside material topics.

Step 8: Set Targets and Action Plans

ESRS requires disclosure of transition plans for climate change. Set science-based targets (SBTi), define decarbonization levers, and disclose governance of climate-related risks. Include Capex and Opex alignment with transition plan.

Step 9: Prepare for Assurance

Limited assurance is required from FY2024. Reasonable assurance from FY2028. Establish internal controls, documentation trails, and data quality procedures. Engage assurance provider early — ideally 3-6 months before reporting deadline.

Step 10: Format and Tag Report

CSRD reports must be in XHTML format with ESRS datapoints tagged using ESEF taxonomy. Prepare digital report that is machine-readable. Consider using specialized CSRD reporting software or working with a service provider.

Step 11: Board Approval and Publication

Sustainability report must be approved by management body and published as part of management report. File with national business register. Ensure consistency with financial statements and other public disclosures.

Step 12: Continuous Improvement

Annual materiality reassessment. Update data collection processes. Respond to assurance findings. Track regulatory updates (EFRAG is continuously updating ESRS). Benchmark against peers and best practice.
