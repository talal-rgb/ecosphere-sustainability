Carbon Footprinting FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions about carbon footprinting.

What is a carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (GHG) emitted directly or indirectly by an individual, organization, event, or product, expressed in carbon dioxide equivalents (CO₂e). Organizational footprints cover Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions. Product footprints cover the full lifecycle from raw materials to disposal.

What is the difference between organizational and product carbon footprint?

Organizational carbon footprint measures all emissions of a company or entity. Product carbon footprint (PCF) measures emissions of a single product throughout its lifecycle. Organizations need both: the organizational footprint for corporate reporting (CSRD, CDP) and the PCF for product claims and customer requirements.

Which standard should I use for carbon footprinting?

For organizational footprints, use the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard (most widely used) or ISO 14064-1 (for verification). For product footprints, use ISO 14067 (international standard) or GHG Protocol Product Standard. PAS 2050 is also used, particularly in the UK.

What data do I need for a carbon footprint?

Energy bills, fuel purchases, transport records, material quantities, waste data, and employee travel. For Scope 3, you need supplier data, which is often the biggest challenge. Terrnx provides data collection templates and supplier engagement support.
