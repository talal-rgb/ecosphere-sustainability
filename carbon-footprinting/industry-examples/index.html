Carbon Footprinting Industry Examples

Sector-specific scenarios showing how carbon footprinting works across different industries.

Manufacturing

Complex Scope 1 from combustion and industrial processes. Scope 2 from facility electricity. Scope 3 from raw materials typically dominates at 60-80% of total. Key challenge: collecting supplier emissions data for thousands of components. Terrnx approach: prioritize top 20% of suppliers by spend, use product-level LCA data where available.

Retail

Low direct emissions (Scope 1: 5-10% from stores and fleet). Store electricity is major Scope 2 source. Scope 3 dominates at 75-85%: purchased goods, upstream transport, product use, and end-of-life. Key challenge: thousands of SKUs with different supply chains. Terrnx approach: category-level screening, then deep-dive on high-volume products.

Technology / Software

Very low direct emissions (Scope 1: 1-3% from offices). Data centers and cloud infrastructure drive Scope 2 and Scope 3. Hardware manufacturing (laptops, servers, network equipment) is major Scope 3 category. Employee commuting and home working increasingly material. Terrnx approach: focus on data center energy and hardware lifecycle.

Logistics & Transport

Fleet fuel dominates Scope 1 (60-80% for road transport). Warehouses contribute Scope 2. Purchased transport services are Scope 3. Key challenge: allocating emissions to specific customers or shipments. Terrnx approach: activity-based allocation using tonne-km data, shift to low-emission vehicles.

Food & Agriculture

Complex biological emissions: methane from livestock, N2O from fertilizers. Land use change can be major emission source. Processing and refrigeration add energy emissions. Packaging and transport are significant Scope 3. Terrnx approach: IPCC Tier 2/3 methods for biological emissions, farm-level data collection.

Construction

Cement and steel are carbon-intensive materials (Scope 3). On-site equipment fuel is Scope 1. Transport of materials to site is significant. Building operational energy (use phase) often exceeds construction emissions over lifetime. Terrnx approach: whole-life carbon assessment, material substitution analysis.
