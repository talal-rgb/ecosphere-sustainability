Product Carbon Footprint Guide

A practical guide to calculating product carbon footprints aligned with ISO 14067 and GHG Protocol Product Standard.

What is a PCF? The total greenhouse gas emissions caused by a product throughout its lifecycle, expressed in kg CO₂e. Required by EU Green Claims Directive, increasingly requested by B2B customers, and essential for credible environmental marketing.

Step 1: Define Goal and Scope

Determine why you need the PCF: customer requirement, regulatory compliance, eco-design, or marketing claim? Define the functional unit (e.g., "1 kg of product" or "1 unit used for 5 years"). Choose system boundary: cradle-to-gate (raw materials to factory gate) or cradle-to-grave (full lifecycle including use and disposal).

Step 2: Build the Bill of Materials

List all raw materials and components with quantities. Include packaging. Gather supplier data where available. For missing data, use LCA databases (Ecoinvent, GaBi, EF database). Document data sources and quality ratings.

Step 3: Map the Manufacturing Process

Document each production step: energy inputs (electricity, gas, steam), process emissions, water use, waste generation. Allocate emissions between co-products using physical or economic allocation. Include transport between process steps.

Step 4: Calculate Distribution Emissions

Include transport from factory to customer. Use actual transport modes and distances where possible. Apply emission factors from GLEC Framework or national sources. For global distribution, model typical transport routes.

Step 5: Model Use Phase (if cradle-to-grave)

Estimate energy consumption during product use. Model typical usage patterns and lifetime. Include maintenance and replacement parts. This is often the largest phase for energy-using products.

Step 6: Calculate End-of-Life

Model disposal scenario: landfill, incineration, recycling, or composting. Apply waste-specific emission factors. Credit recycling using avoided burden or recycled content approach per ISO 14067. Document allocation method.
