Major ESG Frameworks Explained

GRI (Global Reporting Initiative)

The most widely used sustainability reporting standard. Focuses on impact materiality — how the organization affects the economy, environment, and people. Voluntary but referenced by stock exchanges worldwide.

SASB (Sustainability Accounting Standards Board)

Industry-specific standards focused on financially material sustainability issues. Now part of the IFRS Foundation. Best for investor-facing disclosures.

TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures)

Four-pillar framework: Governance, Strategy, Risk Management, Metrics & Targets. Now integrated into ISSB standards. Required by many regulators and investors.

ISSB (International Sustainability Standards Board)

Develops IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards. IFRS S1 (general) and S2 (climate) create a global baseline. Adopted by UK, Australia, Singapore, and others.

CSRD / ESRS (EU)

Mandatory for ~50,000 EU companies. Double materiality: both impact and financial materiality. 12 ESRS standards covering environment, social, and governance.
