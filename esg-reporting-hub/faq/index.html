ESG Reporting Hub FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions about esg reporting hub.

What is ESG reporting?

ESG reporting is the disclosure of environmental, social, and governance data by companies to stakeholders including investors, regulators, customers, and employees. It covers topics like climate impact, workforce diversity, human rights, board structure, and business ethics.

Which ESG framework should I use?

It depends on your stakeholders and jurisdiction. EU companies typically need CSRD/ESRS. US companies may need SEC climate disclosure. Global companies often use ISSB or GRI for broader impact reporting. Many companies use multiple frameworks. Terrnx helps you choose and align.

Is ESG reporting mandatory?

Yes for many companies. CSRD covers ~50,000 EU companies. SEC climate rules affect US registrants. Many stock exchanges require ESG disclosure for listed companies. Even where voluntary, investors and customers increasingly demand it.

How much does ESG reporting cost?

First-year costs range from EUR 30,000 to EUR 500,000+ depending on company size, framework complexity, and data maturity. Ongoing annual costs are typically 30-50% of first-year costs. Terrnx reduces costs by 40-60% through automation and efficient processes.

What is the difference between GRI and SASB?

GRI focuses on impact materiality — how the organization affects the economy, environment, and people. SASB focuses on financial materiality — sustainability issues that affect financial performance. GRI is broader; SASB is investor-focused. They are complementary.

What is ISSB?

The International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB) develops IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards (IFRS S1 and S2). It creates a global baseline for sustainability-related financial disclosures. Adopted by UK, Australia, Singapore, Nigeria, and others. Converges with TCFD recommendations.
