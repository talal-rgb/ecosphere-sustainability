ISO 14064 FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions about iso 14064.

What is ISO 14064?

ISO 14064 is an international standard for greenhouse gas accounting and verification. It has three parts: Part 1 for organizational GHG inventories, Part 2 for project-level quantification and monitoring, and Part 3 for verification and validation of GHG statements.

Is ISO 14064 mandatory?

No, ISO 14064 is voluntary. However, it is widely recognized and often required by carbon neutrality programmes, CDP, voluntary carbon markets, and some customer requirements. It provides credibility that voluntary claims often lack.

How much does ISO 14064 verification cost?

Verification costs typically range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 30,000+ depending on organization size, complexity, and scope covered. Preparation costs (building inventory, documentation) are additional. Terrnx reduces preparation costs by 50-60% through efficient processes.

What is the difference between ISO 14064 and GHG Protocol?

GHG Protocol provides guidance on how to calculate emissions. ISO 14064 provides standards for quantification, reporting, and verification. They are complementary: use GHG Protocol for methodology, ISO 14064 for auditability, certification, and verification.

How long does ISO 14064 verification take?

Preparation: 4-12 weeks depending on data availability. Verification process: 2-6 weeks. Total timeline: 2-4 months for first-time verification. Annual re-verification is faster: 2-4 weeks. Terrnx reduces preparation time through templates and pre-validation.

Who can verify ISO 14064?

Only accredited verification bodies can issue ISO 14064 verification statements. Accreditation is under ISO 14065. Major providers include SGS, Bureau Veritas, TÜV Rheinland, BSI, DNV, and Intertek. Always check the verifier's scope of accreditation.
