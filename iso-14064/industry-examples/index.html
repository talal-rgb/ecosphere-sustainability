ISO 14064 Industry Examples

Sector-specific scenarios showing how iso 14064 works across different industries.

Manufacturing

Complex Scope 1 from combustion and industrial processes. Process emissions (cement, chemicals, steel) require specialized monitoring. Scope 3 from raw materials is typically 60-80% of total. Verification focuses on process emissions data quality and emission factor selection. Terrnx approach: CEMS integration, supplier data programmes.

Energy & Power Generation

Power generators use ISO 14064-1 for corporate inventory and 14064-2 for renewable energy project quantification. Both are critical for green certificates and carbon credits. Fuel mix and grid factors are key variables. Terrnx approach: real-time fuel monitoring, grid emission factor updates.

Logistics & Transport

Fleet fuel dominates Scope 1. Purchased transport services are Scope 3. Verification checks fuel consumption records, transport emission factors, and allocation methods. Multi-modal transport requires careful attribution. Terrnx approach: telematics integration, modal shift analysis.

Agriculture & Food

Methane and N2O from livestock and fertilizers create complex Scope 1. Biological carbon sequestration may be quantified under ISO 14064-2. Land use change emissions are significant. Verification requires agricultural expertise. Terrnx approach: farm-level data, IPCC Tier 2/3 methods, soil carbon monitoring.

Real Estate & Construction

Building operational energy (Scope 2) and onsite fuel (Scope 1). Embodied carbon in materials is Scope 3. Green building certifications (LEED, BREEAM) often require ISO 14064-aligned inventories. Terrnx approach: building energy management, embodied carbon databases.

Waste Management

Landfill methane is major Scope 1. Waste-to-energy processes have complex emissions profiles. Recycling creates avoided emissions (Scope 3). ISO 14064-2 is used for landfill gas capture projects. Terrnx approach: gas monitoring, waste composition analysis, project-level quantification.
