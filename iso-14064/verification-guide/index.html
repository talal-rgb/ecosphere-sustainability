ISO 14064 Verification Guide

Everything you need to prepare for and pass ISO 14064 third-party verification.

Why verify? Third-party verification adds credibility to GHG claims, satisfies CDP and CSRD requirements, supports carbon neutrality programmes, and is often required by customers and investors. ISO 14064-3 defines the verification standard.

Step 1: Build Your GHG Inventory

Follow ISO 14064-1 to quantify all Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions. Document organizational boundaries, operational boundaries, base year, and methodologies. Ensure emission factors are from reputable sources (IPCC, DEFRA, EPA, national inventories). Document all data sources and calculation spreadsheets.

Step 2: Establish Data Management

Implement quality control procedures, data validation checks, and record-keeping systems. Ensure traceability from raw data (invoices, meter readings) to reported emissions. Assign data owners for each emission source. Document change control procedures for methodology updates.

Step 3: Assess Uncertainty

Quantify uncertainty for key data sources and calculations. ISO 14064 requires explicit consideration of uncertainty. Use error propagation or Monte Carlo simulation for complex inventories. Document uncertainty ranges and their impact on total emissions. High uncertainty may limit the level of assurance achievable.

Step 4: Select a Verification Body

Choose an accredited verification body with ISO 14065 accreditation. Check scope of accreditation covers your sector and GHG programme. Request quotes from 2-3 bodies. Typical verification costs: EUR 5,000-30,000 depending on organization size and complexity. Timeline: 2-6 weeks.

Step 5: Prepare Evidence

Compile all supporting documentation organized by scope category: energy invoices, fuel purchase records, transport logs, process emission data, emission factor sources, calculation spreadsheets, and organizational boundary maps. Create an evidence index mapping each reported figure to its supporting data.

Step 6: Undergo Verification

The verifier conducts strategic analysis (risk assessment), process analysis (methodology review), and substantive testing (data verification). Be prepared for site visits, interviews with data owners, and detailed calculation checks. Respond promptly to information requests.

Step 7: Receive Statement

Upon successful verification, you receive a verification statement confirming the inventory meets ISO 14064-1 requirements. Address any findings or non-conformities. The statement will specify the level of assurance (reasonable or limited) and any qualifications or exclusions.
