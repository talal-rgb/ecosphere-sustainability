A comprehensive guide to ISO 14064 standards for quantifying, reporting, and verifying greenhouse gas emissions. From inventory build to third-party verification.

Why ISO 14064 Matters

ISO 14064 provides internationally recognized standards for quantifying, monitoring, reporting, and verifying GHG emissions. It underpins carbon neutrality claims, CDP submissions, CSRD compliance, and voluntary carbon markets. Unlike the GHG Protocol which provides guidance, ISO 14064 provides auditable standards.

ISO 14064 in Context

ISO 14064 vs GHG Protocol

GHG Protocol provides guidance on how to calculate emissions. ISO 14064 provides standards for quantification, reporting, and verification. They are complementary: use GHG Protocol for methodology, ISO 14064 for auditability and certification.

ISO 14064 vs ISO 14067

ISO 14064 covers organizational and project-level GHG. ISO 14067 covers product-level carbon footprints. Companies often need both: 14064 for corporate reporting, 14067 for product claims.

When You Need It

Carbon neutrality claims, CDP submissions, CSRD compliance, voluntary carbon credit projects, supplier requirements, investor requests, and green bond issuance.

Verification Bodies

Accredited bodies include SGS, Bureau Veritas, TÜV Rheinland, BSI, DNV, and Intertek. Accreditation is under ISO 14065. Always verify the body's scope of accreditation.
