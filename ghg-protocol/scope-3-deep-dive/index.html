Scope 3 Deep Dive

Master Scope 3 emissions with practical guidance on data collection, supplier engagement, and reduction strategies.

The Scope 3 Challenge: Scope 3 often represents 70-90% of total emissions but is the hardest to measure. It requires data from suppliers, customers, and partners — most of whom have never calculated emissions before. CSRD and SBTi both require Scope 3 disclosure.

Supplier Engagement Strategy

A structured approach to collecting supplier emissions data:

  1. Prioritize: Rank suppliers by spend × emission intensity. Focus on top 20% covering 80% of Scope 3.
  2. Request: Send standardized data request with clear instructions, templates, and deadlines. Use CDP or proprietary questionnaires.
  3. Support: Provide training, calculation tools, and FAQ. Many suppliers lack capability.
  4. Follow up: Set deadlines, send reminders, escalate to procurement for non-responders.
  5. Estimate: Use spend-based or average-data methods where primary data is unavailable. Document assumptions.
  6. Iterate: Improve data quality annually. Move from spend-based to supplier-specific to product-specific.

Data Quality Hierarchy

GHG Protocol ranks data quality from best to acceptable:

Best: Supplier-specific primary data (meter readings, fuel records)

Good: Average product-level data (industry averages by product)

Acceptable: Sector-average data (industry emission factors)

Poor: Spend-based estimates (economic emission factors)

Reduction Strategies

Effective Scope 3 reduction requires supply chain collaboration:

  • Switch suppliers: Prioritize lower-carbon suppliers in procurement decisions. Include emissions in RFP criteria.
  • Redesign products: Use less carbon-intensive materials. Reduce material intensity. Design for recyclability.
  • Optimize logistics: Shift to lower-emission transport modes. Consolidate shipments. Optimize routing.
  • Extend product life: Design for durability, repairability, and upgradability. Reduce replacement frequency.
  • Supplier targets: Require key suppliers to set science-based targets. Include in contracts and scorecards.
