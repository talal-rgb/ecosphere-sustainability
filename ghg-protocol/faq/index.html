GHG Protocol FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions about ghg protocol.

What is the GHG Protocol?

The Greenhouse Gas Protocol is the world's most widely used standard for GHG accounting. Developed by WRI and WBCSD, it provides standardized frameworks to measure and manage greenhouse gas emissions from private and public sector operations, value chains, and mitigation actions.

What are Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions?

Scope 1: Direct emissions from owned or controlled sources (combustion, processes, fugitive). Scope 2: Indirect emissions from purchased energy (electricity, heat, steam). Scope 3: All other indirect emissions in your value chain (purchased goods, transport, waste, business travel, product use, investments).

Is GHG Protocol mandatory?

The GHG Protocol itself is voluntary but is referenced by mandatory regimes including CSRD, CDP, SBTi, and many national regulations. Most corporate sustainability programmes use GHG Protocol as the calculation foundation.

How do I calculate Scope 3 emissions?

Scope 3 has 15 categories. Start by screening all categories using spend-based estimates. Prioritize categories with highest emissions and strategic relevance. Collect primary data from suppliers for priority categories. Use GHG Protocol Scope 3 Guidance and sector-specific guidance.

What emission factors should I use?

Use official national emission factors where available (DEFRA for UK, EPA for US, ADEME for France). For international consistency, use IPCC factors or the GHG Protocol Emission Factors database. For product-level calculations, use LCA databases like Ecoinvent or GaBi.
