GHG Protocol Industry Examples

Sector-specific scenarios showing how ghg protocol works across different industries.

Manufacturing

Scope 1: 15-25% (combustion, industrial processes). Scope 2: 10-20% (facility electricity). Scope 3: 55-75% (raw materials, purchased components, transport, product use). Key challenge: process emissions from cement, chemicals, and steel. Terrnx approach: site-level monitoring, supplier engagement for materials.

Financial Services

Scope 1: 1-2% (offices). Scope 2: 2-5% (office electricity). Scope 3: 93-97% — predominantly Category 15 (investments and loans, i.e., financed emissions). This is the hardest category to calculate. PCAF standards provide methodology. Terrnx approach: portfolio emissions platform, asset-level data integration.

Retail

Scope 1: 5-10% (stores, delivery fleet). Scope 2: 10-15% (store electricity). Scope 3: 75-85% (purchased goods, upstream transport, product use, end-of-life). Refrigerant leakage (F-gases) can be significant Scope 1. Terrnx approach: category-level screening, focus on private-label products.

Software / Technology

Scope 1: 1-3% (offices). Scope 2: 10-20% (data centers, offices — often 100% renewable). Scope 3: 77-89% (hardware manufacturing, cloud infrastructure, employee commuting, home working). Cloud provider emissions are often the largest category. Terrnx approach: cloud carbon tools, hardware lifecycle assessment.

Aviation

Scope 1: 95%+ (jet fuel combustion). Scope 2: minimal (airport electricity). Scope 3: passenger transport to airports, aircraft manufacturing, maintenance. SAF (sustainable aviation fuel) is key decarbonization lever. Terrnx approach: fuel monitoring, SAF procurement strategy, passenger transport surveys.

Real Estate

Scope 1: 10-20% (onsite fuel, fugitive emissions). Scope 2: 20-40% (purchased electricity). Scope 3: 40-70% (tenant energy, construction, maintenance, embodied carbon in materials). Whole-building approach is essential. Terrnx approach: building energy management, embodied carbon in renovations, green lease structures.
